One more thing

Projects are only one part of getting hired. Employers also look at:

Clear GitHub repositories with good READMEs.
Clean, modular code.
Understanding of ML concepts (not just using libraries).
Ability to explain design decisions in interviews.
Basic software engineering practices (testing, version control, documentation).

If you build a few high-quality, well-documented projects and can confidently discuss why you made certain choices, you'll be in a much stronger position than someone with dozens of tutorial-based repositories.
